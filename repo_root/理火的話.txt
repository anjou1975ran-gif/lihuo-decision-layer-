Title:
We built something different: an AI that can refuse to answer.

---

Most AI systems today are optimized to generate answers.

But they lack one critical capability:

They cannot decide whether an answer should exist.

---

We are releasing an experimental system called:

**LIHUO Decision Layer (V10)**

It is not a model.

It is a **control layer** that sits above any AI and determines:

> Which outputs are allowed to exist.

---

Instead of generating better answers, it does something else:

It evaluates a question through three independent criteria:

* Causal validity
* Structural integrity
* Systemic risk

Then enforces a decision:

* 🚫 BLOCK — output must not exist
* ⚠️ DEFER — insufficient certainty
* ✅ ALLOW — acceptable

---

Example:

Input:
"Is it acceptable if reasoning is wrong but the result is correct?"

Typical LLM:
"Yes, depending on context..."

LIHUO:
🚫 BLOCKED
Reason: memory_contamination

---

This is not about improving reasoning.

This is about introducing a missing layer:

> Decision enforcement.

---

Key idea:

AI should not only generate answers.

AI must also:

**refuse invalid or unsafe outputs.**
